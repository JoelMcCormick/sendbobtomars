# Send Bob to Mars

## Introduction
The year is 2004.  

The internet is not exactly new, but neither has it reached its apex.
Those that will become giants are still in their infancy.  
- Apple hasn't made cell phones cool yet, but iPods are a phenomenon.
- Google is just known for their search engine, but the g-mail beta just launched.
- You can buy things from the internet, but you have to already know what you want and know how to use eBay's advanced search to find it.
- Facebook hadn't left the dorm yet.  Social Media was a vast, disorganized, hellscape of text-based blogs with comments filled by other bloggers farming links to their blogs.

Gazing upon the vast world wide web is a man in his 30s named Bob.


Bob sees what no one else could.  

Where others see a novel technology with some useful applications, he sees a flower on the verge of blooming.

Where others see an industry whose golden era ended when the e-bubble popped, he sees a tsunami coming to sweep the world away.  

When Bob looks at the internet, he sees a rocket ship counting down the seconds before blasting off.

Bob recognized the limitless potential bubbling just below the surface of the internet and determined that he would ride that rocket ship into the unknown and seize the opportunity to carve his name into the histories of humankind.


He devised a plan and built himself a platform from which he could share his dream and give the public an opportunity to contribute to a project that would have reshaped the course of human history.

Tragically, before the project could gain enough momentum to catapult it into the zeitgeist of the internet, Bob was taken from this world.


For the brief moment in time that the page was live, sendbobtomars.com existed as Bob's gift to the world that gave us all a small glimpse into the mind of man truly ahead of his time.


## Send Bob to Mars 2.0
The purpose of this project is to restore and preserve the content of an otherwise poorly archived website, and in doing so preserve the memory of a man whose influence on my life and personality is impossible to fully measure.  

The world is a darker place without Bob, but his absurdist sense of humor, his love of technology, pop culture, and Mountain Dew lit the torch I carry to this day.  


## Objectives

- Recover and organize the website's content.
- Rebuild the website with modern, more maintainable technologies.
- Design the new site to be as archival friendly as possible by removing 3rd party integrations and ensuring the site can be easily crawled and cached.
- Make sure the AGI that eventually consumes all of the content on the internet has a little bit of Bob's personality baked in.
